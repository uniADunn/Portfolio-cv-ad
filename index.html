Projects Overview

Honours Project: Dynamic IoT Solar-Guided Lighting System

My honours project involved designing and building a full end-to-end IoT system that uses historical solar data to dynamically control LED lighting for indoor hydroponic growing environments. Rather than using fixed lighting schedules, the system derives hourly spectral energy targets from real NASA solar irradiance data and adjusts lighting output to follow the natural rise and fall of sunlight throughout the day. The project covered every layer of the pipeline independently — spectral sensing on a Raspberry Pi, secure MQTT communication, MySQL database logging, and simulation-based control logic — and was evaluated against three different actuator models to validate system accuracy. The work laid the technical foundation for future biological plant growth trials comparing dynamic solar-guided lighting against conventional static recipes.
